Carissa believes that dance is more than choreography and music. For her, it's a moment where her body and soul move freely through time and space. From a young age, Carissa has always had a strong connection with dance. She began her journey at local suburban studios such as Dorothy's Dancing, Northwest Ballet Academy in Schaumburg, and Kristina's Studio of Dance in Buffalo Grove. After learning the core styles such as tap, jazz, hiphop, and ballet, she started exploring higher elements of dance. She was on the orchesis dance team while in high school and began choreographing routines for the student shows and sports games.

Carissa had the opportunity to continue to study dance while attending Carthage College in Kenosha, Wisconsin. She was introduced to the history of dance and the creation of choreography throughout her coursework. During her sophomore year of school, she studied in the city of Merida, located in the Yucatan peninsula of Mexico. Carissa was introduced to a local studio owner who pushed her technical boundaries with Spanish ballet and styles such as Merengue, Salsa and Cumbia.

In 2011, her learning and inspiration continued to grow. Carissa was recognized by the Carthage Theater Department for "Outstanding Achievement in Choreography and Composition" for her Bollywood piece. In 2013, she was able to see Bollywood first hand by traveling to India for a January study course. That same year, she completed her Bachelor's of Arts degree and a minor in Dance.

Carissa loves traveling, inspiring others, and building new relationships. Carissa continues her love for dance by completing a variety of fitness certifications. She enjoys teaching classes and finding new ways to explore the beautiful world of dance.
